## The Mindset Shift: AI as Co-Worker, Not Search Engine

Using AI as a search engine is like hiring a PhD economist to fetch your coffee. The tool is capable of vastly more than most people ask of it. The shift is from "let me ask AI a question" to "let me delegate this entire task to AI."

Delegation means: giving context, specifying the output format, setting constraints, and reviewing the result — not doing the work yourself.

## The Morning AI Routine

Start every workday with a 15-minute AI session:

1. **Brain dump** — write everything on your mind in 2 minutes
2. **AI structure** — paste it to Claude: "Turn this brain dump into a prioritised to-do list for today. Mark urgent vs important."
3. **Email triage** — copy your inbox subjects to Claude: "Which of these emails require my personal response vs can be delegated or archived?"
4. **Prep key meetings** — "Summarise what I need to know before my 10am call about [topic]"

Result: you start the day with clarity instead of overwhelm.

## Building Personal Automation Pipelines

Level 3 is where the real gains are. Examples:
- New email from client → AI summarises + drafts reply → sent to your Slack for one-click approval
- New lead form submission → AI qualifies + writes personalised response → sent automatically
- Weekly sales data → AI generates narrative report → emailed to team every Monday 8am

Each pipeline saves 2-5 hours per week and runs forever once built.

## Common Mistakes That Kill AI Productivity

1. **Using AI for everything indiscriminately** — AI is slow for simple lookups; use it for complex, time-consuming tasks
2. **Not giving context** — vague prompts produce vague outputs; brief AI like a new employee
3. **Treating first outputs as final** — AI outputs are first drafts; your expertise makes them excellent
4. **Not building a prompt library** — save your best prompts; they're a productivity asset
