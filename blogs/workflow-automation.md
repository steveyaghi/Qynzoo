Every repetitive task your team does manually is a tax on your growth. Email triage, invoice processing, lead follow-ups, report generation, social scheduling — these are not jobs that require human judgment. They are patterns, and AI is extraordinarily good at patterns.

## Step 1: Audit Your Workflows Before You Automate Anything

The biggest mistake businesses make with automation is jumping straight to tools. The right starting point is a workflow audit. For one week, have each person on your team log every repetitive task they perform, how long it takes, and how often.

**The Automation Readiness Test** — ask three questions:
1. Does it happen more than once a week?
2. Could you write down every step without using the word "it depends"?
3. Does it start and end with digital data?

If yes to all three: automate it.

## The 5 Automation Categories with the Fastest ROI

- **Lead Response** — respond to new enquiries within 5 minutes, 24/7, using AI qualification
- **Invoice & Payment** — generate, send, and chase invoices automatically
- **Reporting** — pull data, compile reports, distribute to stakeholders on schedule
- **Client Onboarding** — trigger welcome sequences, create folders, assign tasks at contract signing
- **Content Distribution** — publish once, auto-share across all platforms

## Choosing the Right Tools: Make vs n8n vs Zapier

- **Zapier** — easiest, 6,000+ integrations, best for simple 2-3 step automations. Gets expensive at scale.
- **Make** — visual, powerful, 1,800+ integrations, handles complex logic. Qynzoo's default recommendation for SMEs.
- **n8n** — open-source, self-hostable, cheapest at high volumes. Requires more technical setup.

## How to Implement Your First Automation

1. **Map the workflow on paper first** — write every step before opening any tool
2. **Build with sample data** — never test against production data
3. **Add error handling from day one** — build notification paths for when things break
4. **Run parallel for two weeks** — compare automated vs manual output before cutting over
5. **Measure and document** — track time saved, document the workflow, build your automation library

## Adding AI to Your Automation

A real example: when a prospect fills a contact form, Make sends the text to Claude via API. Claude classifies the lead (hot/warm/cold), extracts key info, and writes a personalised response. Make creates a CRM entry, logs in Notion, sends the response, and notifies the sales team in Slack. Total time: under 90 seconds. Previously: 2–3 hours per lead.

## Common Mistakes

- **Automating a broken process** — fix the process first, then automate
- **No error handling** — silent failures are the most dangerous kind
- **Over-engineering the first version** — start simple, add complexity incrementally
- **No human override** — every automation touching customers or money needs a human review path for exceptions
