# Content Improvements — Readability, Clarity & Marketing Audit
**Date:** March 1, 2026  
**Version:** 3.14 → 3.14.2  
**Scope:** All public-facing HTML pages (11 files)

---

## Summary

A full content audit was performed across all pages. The site had solid technical structure but suffered from **5 critical marketing weaknesses**:

1. **Uncommitted service copy** — e.g. *"Contact me to look for possibilities"* on the Local AI card
2. **Feature-led language** — services described what they *are*, not what they *do for the client*
3. **Generic CTAs** — "Get Started Today" / "Schedule a Consultation" with no urgency or specificity
4. **Bland section headers** — corporate filler with no emotional or outcome-driven pull
5. **Repeated identical footer copy** — same placeholder text on every page

## Marketing Principles Applied

### Pain-First Copywriting
Every service and CTA now leads with the **client's problem** before presenting the solution. Visitors immediately recognise their situation and keep reading.

### Specific Over Generic
CTAs name specific deliverables: *"30-minute workflow audit"*, *"identify the 3 processes"*, *"2–3 tools that will deliver the biggest ROI"* — not vague promises.

### Outcome-Focused Language
Service cards describe **what the client gains**, not what the service is. E.g. Local AI no longer says "has AI models" — it says "AI power that never leaves your control."

### Consistent Brand Voice
All 11 pages (including legal) now share the same footer description. The story Qynzoo tells is identical everywhere a new visitor lands.

### Urgency & Specificity in CTAs  
Every CTA implies a low-risk, high-value first step (free consultation, free audit, free demo). Each is tied directly to the article topic — not a generic "get started."
